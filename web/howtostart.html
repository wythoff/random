HOW TO START?

So, you’ve decided you want to start programming — you’re excited and ready to learn, and then you ask yourself, “Where do I begin, and which language do I start with?” The answer to that question is a little tough, and it’s certainly something everyone struggles with when one is starting out.

This is what ALGRANTH is for, to start to develop your interest in coding by providing a platform where you can find out a vast number of algorithms taught in a a simpler and better way. We help you develop your problem solving skills by providing the most efficient solution to a certain problem. We aim to create a platform where everyone joins, to form a community of coders.

Once you are handson with a certain language you can start off with competitive coding.And by the end of this, we have also provided some list of sites where you could actually start off with competitve coding.

What is competitive coding?

Competitive coding is a sport where you are provided with a problem statement, generally logical or mathematical and you need to generalise it and code so that the computer solves it for you.

Why start competitive coding?

Competitive coding helps you increase your problem solving skills and increases your logic. You learn to find the most efficient solution for a certain problem. It helps you improve your debugging skills. You find out that in some cases your answer is right while in some it gets wrong. This helps you braoaden your thinking capabilities. Also it adds up to your skillset as a plus point in your resume.

What is the best language to start with?

This has got to be the number one thing we hear people worrying about when deciding to start programming. You might be worried about which language is the most used in industries, and it wont help you in a longer term. Some languages are powerful, some are fast, and some are industry standards, but none of it matters to you when you start. What does matter is starting to think like a programmer.

Programmers are problem solvers,language is only a medium to solve those problems. you need not worry about the language at starting — what’s much more important is getting used to thinking differently, being open, and being ready to learn from your mistakes. Our main advice for anyone wanting to start is to do exactly that — just start.

As soon as you get your hands dirty and actually write some code and get it running, you will already have gained an enormous amount of knowledge. Don’t be afraid to make mistakes — because those will teach you more than your correct solutions. It can actually be an extremely fun experience to try out new things.
